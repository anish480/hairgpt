# Moxie — Brand Guidelines for Content & Scripting

## 1. Standing Rules

Explicitly stated by reviewers as broad principles — not feedback on one script.


### Brand & Messaging

- Avoid the phrase 'harsh chemicals'; find alternative phrasing to convey the message without demonizing chemicals.
  _— Nikita_

- Do not use negative words to describe hair types.
  _— Nikita_

- Maintain a positive and factual brand voice, even when a creator uses self-deprecating terms in their personal voice.
  _— Nikita_

- Reserve the narrative of a 'surface-level temporary fix' exclusively for the HA (Hydrorepair) product line.
  _— Nikita_


### Copywriting

- Do not use value judgments like 'Ghosla' and 'Hagrid' in brand copy.
  _— Nikita_

- Use factual hair descriptors like 'poofy' and 'frizz'.
  _— Nikita_

- Use colloquial or negative terms only in a 'myth-busting' context, followed by an immediate brand refutation.
  _— Nikita_

- Use the phrase 'set it forget it' in scripts where applicable.
  _— Nainan_

- Avoid the acronym 'CGM'. Either spell it out as 'curly girl method' for clarity or reframe as a generic 'curly routine' for broader audience relatability.
  _— Nikita_

- Use the term 'crunchy' only when space is limited and simpler words like 'hard and sticky' are not feasible.
  _— Nikita_


### Product Clarity

- Reserve the 'repair from within' benefit specifically for the Hydrorepair routine and Hydrorepair mask.
  _— Nikita_

- For all Dry Shampoo ads, emphasize that the product is 'very safe' and has 'no chalky white cast' as critical messages.
  _— Nikita_

- All Dry Shampoo scripts must include the directive to 'blend it well' as a key usage instruction.
  _— Nikita_


### Audience (Men)

- For male-targeted copy, focus on outcomes like 'hair being set, looking good, proper, or managed' rather than technical terms.
  _— Seikhohao_

- Recognize that men often use slang to describe their hair due to unfamiliarity with technical terms.
  _— Sahil_


### Process & Research

- Monitor comment sections on relevant social media platforms to identify authentic consumer language.
  _— Nikita_

- When consulting with Sahil, ask tactical questions about problem articulation and consumer psychology; do not treat him as a writer.
  _— Nikita_

## 2. General Copy & Script Guidelines

Recurring directives that apply across multiple products.


### Copy & Tone

- Rephrase 'when I switched to' to be more conversational; consider alternatives like 'and honestly it’s a lifesaver'.
  _— Nikita · Instagram reel reference_

- Use 'hard and sticky' instead of 'crunchy' as it is more relatable for an Indian audience.
  _— Nikita_

- Write superlative and impactful CTAs, such as 'this is me not washing my hair but still looking like a diva'.
  _— Nikita_

- Ensure hooks are simple and direct to prevent audience drop-off.
  _— Nikita_


### Audience Targeting

- Simplify language and content for male audiences; avoid technical terms like 'hydration' and 'frizz' that may not be relatable.
  _— Nikita · Ana Mishraa DS script v3 Option 2_


### Script Structure & Concept

- Frame product benefits by first addressing the fundamental problem (e.g., moisturizing dry hair) before mentioning advanced benefits (e.g., not weighing hair down).
  _— Nikita_

- Move script narratives beyond the 'discovery' phase to detail the product's specific role and benefits.
  _— Nikita_

- Ensure scripts clearly explain what a product or routine does and its specific outcome.
  _— Nikita_


### Brand & Messaging

- Position Moxie as part of a creator's hair journey, not the sole 'game changer', to maintain authenticity.
  _— Nikita_

## 3. Product-Specific Guidelines

Requirements and rules specific to each product line.


### Curly

**Copy**

- Avoid the term 'frizz' in men's curly copy, as it is considered too technical and associated with long hair.
  _— Nikita · Kartik Curly Men NV Script_

- Simplify hooks for men's curly hair content to be more direct and 'boy friendly', using examples like 'If you want great curls like mine'.
  _— Nikita · Beven Curly script_

- Use 'curl care' instead of more technical phrases like 'layering hydration'.
  _— Nikita · Sarah Curly Routine script v1_

- End scripts for Indian creators by explicitly stating that Moxie products are made for Indian wavy and curly hair.
  _— Nikita · Namratha Curly Script v1_

**Structure**

- For non-verbal educational concepts, use direct and explicit opening copy to ensure product understanding.
  _— Nikita · Anushka Parnami Curly Non-Verbal Concept: Half & Half_

- For scripts with a dramatic visual transformation, show the before-and-after immediately after the hook.
  _— Nikita · Himika Bose Curly Script with Hook Options_


### Dry Shampoo

**Product Clarity & Safety**

- Move the safety message early in Dry Shampoo scripts and rephrase it to directly address consumer fears about hair loss, rather than using generic phrases like 'safe and gentle'.
  _— Nikita · Ana Mishraa Dry Shampoo Script: Upside Down_

- Emphasize product safety by describing it as a 'completely safe and natural plant powder'.
  _— Nikita · Nandni Dry Shampoo script_

- Instruct users to 'apply it to my roots and blend it in well' to ensure proper usage and prevent complaints about white cast.
  _— Nikita · Tania Moxie HWS Non-verbal video copy_

**Copy & Pacing**

- When describing 'day 3 hair', explicitly state 'greasy and flat' to ensure visual clarity.
  _— Nikita · Ana Mishraa Dry Shampoo Script: Upside Down_

- Introduce the Dry Shampoo product early in the script (before 20 seconds) and remove redundancies to improve pacing.
  _— Nikita · Ana Mishraa Dry Shampoo Script: Upside Down_


### DandruffMCT

**Product Clarity**

- Clearly articulate the innovation and superior effectiveness of the pre-wash format for removing dandruff.
  _— Nikita · Beven DandruffMCT script_

- Always explicitly mention MCT oil as the key ingredient responsible for rebuilding the scalp barrier.
  _— Nikita · Savar DandruffMCT Video Ad Script_

**Copy**

- Avoid phrasing like 'holy grail for dandruff'. Instead, use 'skincare coded' language to describe MCT oil's benefits without implying it is a direct cure.
  _— Nikita · Savar DandruffMCT video ad script v2_


### Heat Protection Spray

**Copy**

- When describing heat-damaged hair, use terms like 'fried' or 'dry' instead of 'poofy'.
  _— Nikita · Medha HPS script_

**Structure**

- Keep scripts for Heat Protection Spray short and concise to maintain audience attention.
  _— Nikita · Medha HPS script_


### Wavy

**Copy & Terminology**

- Always use the full, correct product names: 'leave-in conditioner' (not 'leave-in') and 'serum gel' (not 'gel').
  _— Nikita · Gupil Wavy script v2_

- Always specify 'wavy hair' in hooks and copy to clearly identify the target audience (e.g., 'wavy hair needs...' instead of 'your hair needs...').
  _— Nikita · Gupil Wavy script v2_

- Use the term 'wavy hair' for clarity and avoid repetitive use of 'waves'.
  _— Nikita · Ananya Wavy Script v1_

- Avoid over-emphasizing a 'model' angle to prevent the tone from sounding 'douchey'. Rephrase copy like 'non negotiable ex model routine' to 'non negotiable routine'.
  _— Nikita · Sahib Wavy script v1_

**Product Clarity**

- When mentioning the serum gel, add a disclaimer that hair does not feel hard and the product does not cause hairfall.
  _— Nikita · Gupil Wavy script v2_
